The long version

// cat ~/story.md

Grew up in a Danish village of two thousand. Figured I was destined for big business in the big city — which, for someone from the countryside, was Copenhagen. Got there, enrolled at CBS, and pretty quickly discovered I was bored. Three years of side jobs, side projects, and extracurriculars trying to manufacture the challenge the degree wasn't giving me. In hindsight, I should have studied hardware engineering.

What rescued it was stumbling on the time-value-of-money — a single idea that rewired how I saw everything financial. That momentum carried me to Wharton on exchange, and then into FIH Partners, Copenhagen's sharpest M&A boutique. Great deals, better peers. Also where it became obvious — watching ChatGPT sit quietly at the edge of every deliverable — that LLMs were going to eat large parts of advisory work.

So I pivoted. Googled "sustainability management technology", landed on the EPFL / IMD / HEC joint programme in Lausanne, saw the mountains in the search results, and signed up. The unspoken reason: I needed out of the big-city-Copenhagen loop, and to focus on one thing — studying, and being alive outside of it.

In the mountains I picked up trail running, hiking, biking, ski touring. Had a blast — and rediscovered what I actually like doing: building things. The barrier to building with AI right now is astonishingly low, and I seem to have an intuition for how the pieces fit together. I'm back to playing with Lego: wiring systems, shipping agents, making things that work.

Recently visited my sister in Dublin. She works in tech; most of her friends are solutions engineers and in technical sales. Talking with them was the first "this is it" moment I've had about a role in a long time — the people side and the backend, building long-term relationships with clients while translating what they actually need back into the product. Not far from product management, honestly. That's the direction I'm pointing.
